Why write?

I've read and loved too many stories to not contribute my own.

Consider the following quote from my favorite book, The Last Unicorn by Peter S. Beagle:

"Your name is a golden bell hung in my heart. I would break my body to pieces to call you once by your name."

Isn't that the most beautiful thing you've ever read? I read lines like that, and I feel overwhelmed when I realize the power language has to evoke emotion. It is a kind of power I want to accumulate, and harness, and affect others the way I have been affected.

I believe with all my heart that each person has a set of things within them that only they can bring into the world. If I painted, and endeavored to make something unique and original regardless of quality, I can bring something into the world that would not have existed unless I willed it to be so.

It is no different with writing, or sculpting, or game design, or music. Because there are unique things within each of us, I think there is a certain responsibility to bring some of it out, in a discipline of your choosing.

Sure, but why writing specifically?

Amongst all the available disciplines, when I see things in the world that 'inspire' me, sentences are what come to mind. Most often they are potential first lines of any number of novels.

Beyond that, the romantization of a writer's lifestyle appeals to me. The idea that great success would bring me the opportunity to live secluded at the base of a mountain, surrounded only by a field full of Forget-Me-Nots.
